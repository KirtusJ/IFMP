# MUTUALIST GEORGISM
## A Unified Theory of Economic Transition

**Version:** 1.0 (Draft)  
**Status:** Theoretical Foundation Document  
**Scope:** This document outlines the macroeconomic logic underlying the IFMP. It draws on two traditions—Georgism and Mutualism—and proposes that they are not separate theories but complementary halves of a single unified framework.

---

### I. A NOTE ON ORIGINS

This framework does not claim to be wholly original. It builds on the work of two earlier traditions:

- **Georgism** (Henry George, *Progress and Poverty*, 1879): The insight that land-rent is unearned and that its socialization would eliminate a primary source of inequality and inefficiency.

- **Mutualism** (Pierre-Joseph Proudhon, *What Is Property?*, 1840): The insight that interest-bearing credit is extractive and that cost-basis mutual credit would eliminate another primary source of inequality and inefficiency.

The proposal here is that these two insights are not alternatives but *complements*. Land-rent and interest are structurally identical: both are forms of unearned extraction from productive labor. A complete system must address both.

**Critical Reframing:** Mutualist Georgism is not about "eliminating" extraction. It is about **redirecting** extraction toward more efficient purposes. The goal is not to abolish rent or interest—it is to redirect their flows so that value reaches its sources (labor, innovation, production capacity) more efficiently. This is a systems-efficiency claim, not a moral claim.

---

### II. THE MULTIPLICATIVE DIVIDEND

The core hypothesis of Mutualist Georgism is that eliminating extractive "leakage"—specifically usury (interest) and land-rent—transforms the supply chain from an additive to a *multiplicative* system.

- **Under conventional finance**, the final price of a good is subject to compounding intermediation at each stage of production:

  \[
  P = \left[ \sum (C_n + L_n) \right] \cdot (1 + r)^n
  \]

  Where \(C_n\) = material costs, \(L_n\) = labor costs, \(r\) = usurious intermediation rate (interest + rent markup), and \(n\) = number of production stages.

- **Under Mutualist Georgism**, the compounding friction is removed, collapsing the price function to:

  \[
  P = \sum (C_n + L_n)
  \]

  This eliminates the exponential drag of rentier intermediation. The "dividend" is not a cash transfer to passive owners, but a *systemic reduction in the labor-hour cost of all reproducible goods*.

**Key Insight:** This is not about "redistributing" wealth. It is about **reducing friction** so that value flows more efficiently to its sources. The compounding friction of interest and rent is a source of system inefficiency. Redirecting these flows is an efficiency optimization.

### III. THE LABOR-SAVING QUOTIENT (LSQ)

Mutualist Georgism proposes that Return on Investment (ROI) be restored to its thermodynamic function: a measure of resource efficiency rather than speculative yield.

In the absence of speculative profit signals, capital allocation might be driven by the **Labor-Saving Quotient (LSQ)** —the ratio of physical output gained per unit of human labor-hour invested:

\[
LSQ = \frac{\Delta \text{Physical Output}}{\Delta \text{Labor Hours}}
\]

The "Return" is not a cash dividend to an idle owner, but a *time-dividend* returned to the producer in the form of reduced working hours or increased discretionary consumption capacity. This might create a virtuous cycle of automation, process optimization, and systemic de-friction—though this remains a hypothesis to be tested.

---

### IV. INTERGENERATIONAL SOLVENCY AND ENTROPY

Mutualist Georgism begins from a modest premise: all civilizations must contend with thermodynamic entropy. Physical reality is finite, and resource throughput is subject to physical limits. The question is not whether to acknowledge this constraint, but how to manage it.

- **Conventional capitalism** attempts to outrun entropy via infinite nominal growth on a finite planet, while extracting surplus value at each intermediation node. This appears to lead to ecological overshoot, underconsumption crises, and structural instability.

- **Mutualist Georgism** proposes to manage entropy not by denying physical limits, but by maximizing Total Factor Productivity (TFP) and minimizing structural friction at every stage of production and distribution. The compounding social dividend—derived from eliminated leakage—might allow material standards of living to improve without requiring perpetual resource extraction growth.

**The system does not depend on high TFP growth.** It is stable across low, moderate, and high technological trajectories. It captures whatever efficiency gains occur and distributes them as time-dividends.

---

### V. THE ULTIMATE TIME-DIVIDEND: POST-LABOR

If the system achieves near-zero marginal cost of production across all Utility Layer goods, labor becomes optional. This does not produce alienation; it produces liberation.

- **UBS** provides material security (no one is desperate).
- **DSM** provides meaning, status, and connection (everyone can pursue what matters to them).
- **LMFs** become optional (people can participate if they want, but they don't have to).
- **Governance** remains distributed and available for those who choose to participate.
- **Surplus** continues to circulate (SDR funds R&D, infrastructure, and cultural activities).

**This is not post-scarcity; it's post-labor.** And post-labor is the logical endpoint of efficiency—the ultimate time-dividend.

### VI. STRUCTURAL DIFFERENTIATION: THE 2008 CRISIS IN RETROSPECT

One way to test the Mutualist Georgist framework is to compare its structural logic with that of the pre-2008 financial system. The 2008 crisis is often understood as a failure of debt-leveraged land-rent speculation—a collapse of asset-price feedback loops disconnected from underlying utility.

Two structural differences appear relevant:

1. **Removal of the Land-Rent Anchor:** By socializing land value via the LVC protocol, Mutualist Georgism removes the "Asset-Price Appreciation" variable from investment calculus. Land becomes a cost-of-use, not a capital-gain vehicle. This might eliminate the debt-overcollateralization that drove housing bubbles.

2. **Productive vs. Destructive Deflation:** In conventional systems, falling prices can trigger "Negative Equity" and insolvency because debt is denominated in nominal terms against volatile collateral. In Mutualist Georgism, credit is issued against *future TFP gains* rather than static asset valuations. If the price of a house falls due to genuine efficiency gains, the borrower's labor-value remains stable or increases; debt service is tied to productive capacity, not collateral mark-to-market. This might render debt easier to liquidate in real terms.

These are hypotheses, not certainties. They are proposed as testable propositions for simulation.

### VII. POPULISM PREVENTION AND THE ALIENATED LABOR CLASS

A key function of the IFMP is to prevent populism by integrating the alienated labor class—those who cannot be tied to the global economy. Capitalism cannot solve this; it replaces the human with automation, producing a class of excluded, resentful people.

The IFMP solves this by:

- **Pooling global surplus** (from LVC, MCC efficiency gains, open-source replication).
- **Distributing it to all labor**, regardless of origin, via UBS and time-dividends.
- **Maintaining infrastructure** (funded by SDR).
- **Preserving cultural autonomy** (DSM).
- **Making immigration positive-sum** (more people = more innovation = more time-dividends).

The result is a system that absorbs demographic change without triggering populist backlash.

**Key Insight:** This is not redistribution. It is **reduction of friction**. The surplus being pooled is not "taken" from anyone; it is value that was previously captured by extraction mechanisms. Redirecting it to its sources reduces system friction and prevents the conditions under which populism emerges.

---

### VIII. EXTRACTION AS FRICTION: A TAXONOMY

IFMP does not abolish extraction. It redirects it.

| Extraction Type | Mechanism | IFMP Redirects To |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wage Extraction** | Employers capture surplus value | LMF worker ownership |
| **Resource Extraction** | Landowners capture rent | LVC public use |
| **Dividend Extraction** | Financiers capture interest | MCC productive investment |
| **Polity Extraction** | Cross-border wealth capture | SDR global coordination |

The goal is not to end extraction. The goal is to **reduce friction** by ensuring that value flows to where it creates the most growth, innovation, and prosperity. When extraction is redirected from passive asset holders to active producers, the system becomes more efficient.

### IX. TESTABLE PROPOSITIONS FOR SIMULATION

The Mutualist Georgist framework translates into the following testable propositions for Agent-Based Modeling (ABM):

1. **TFP Growth Profile:** Under Mutualist Georgism, TFP should exhibit a super-additive (potentially \(n^2\)) growth profile due to open-source protocol replication, compared to linear or sub-linear growth under proprietary IP regimes.

2. **Price Deflation Trajectory:** Real prices of Utility Layer goods should asymptotically approach near-zero marginal cost within T+10 cycles, triggering programmed sectoral decommodification.

3. **Debt Solvency Pattern:** Endogenous credit volumes (MCC) should remain within ±15% of physical output growth, preventing the formation of the speculative credit bubbles that characterize rentier finance.

4. **Populism Prevention:** Under demographic change (immigration surge), populist sentiment should remain low due to UBS, infrastructure maintenance, and cultural autonomy.

5. **Post-Labor Stability:** If labor becomes optional, the system should remain stable and meaningful, with UBS providing security and DSM providing meaning.

6. **The Gradient and "Green":** "Green" is not an endpoint. It is a coordinate on a dynamic gradient. As technology advances, the gradient shifts. As culture evolves, the gradient shifts. The system is designed for continuous evolution, not terminal arrival. The green coordinate (UBS ≥ 0.85, DSM ≥ 0.70, TFP ≥ 2.0) is a reference point for high-stability equilibrium, not a final state.

These propositions are intended as starting points for investigation, not as settled conclusions.

### X. IMPLICATIONS FOR THE BROADER PROJECT

If these propositions hold in simulation, Mutualist Georgism might offer a viable alternative to rentier capitalism. If they do not, the framework would require revision or abandonment. The authors are open to either outcome.
