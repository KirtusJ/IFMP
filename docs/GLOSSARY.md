# GLOSSARY OF TERMS
## Integrated Federated Mutualist Protocol (IFMP)

**Version:** 1.0 (Draft)  
**Status:** Reference Document  
**Scope:** This glossary defines the acronyms and technical terms used throughout the IFMP repository. It is intended as a reference for readers and modelers. For full context, refer to the primary documents.

---

### A

**ABM — Agent-Based Modeling**  
A computational simulation method for modeling complex systems by simulating the interactions of autonomous agents. Used in the IFMP project to test the viability of proposed parameters and governance mechanisms.

**ATD — Algorithmic Temporal Decay**  
A proposed mechanism that reduces an individual's strategic decision-weight over time according to a decay function. The half-life is proposed at 36 months. Intended to prevent the re-emergence of entrenched managerial castes by forcing continuous re-legitimation of strategic influence.

---

### C

**CLT — Community Land Trust**  
A non-profit organization that holds land for community benefit, removing it from the speculative market. CLTs are cited as an existing institutional form that could seed the IFMP transition.

---

### D

**DEAP — Distributed Epistemic Agency Protocol**  
The sociological component of the IFMP. Addresses human agency, managerial alienation, and the social dynamics of a post-rentier economy. Proposes mechanisms for distributing epistemic labor, bifurcating markets, and mitigating populist destabilization.

**DSM — Discretionary Social Market**  
The high-friction, exchange-based market for scarce, subjective, and interpersonal goods. Operates alongside the UBS layer. Provides a site for status-signaling, cultural expression, and craft production. Uses the MCC ledger for exchange.

---

### E

**ESOP — Employee Stock Ownership Plan**  
A plan that gives employees ownership interest in the company they work for. Cited as an existing institutional form that could seed the IFMP transition.

---

### I

**IFMP — Integrated Federated Mutualist Protocol**  
The full protocol architecture described in this repository. A proposal for a post-rentier economic coordination system based on Mutualist Georgist principles.

**IP — Intellectual Property**  
Legal rights over creative works and inventions. In the IFMP, IP restrictions are proposed to be abolished. Technological breakthroughs would function as Global Protocol Updates, replicable across all federated nodes.

**IUL — Infrastructural Utility Layer** *(See UBS)*

---

### L

**LMF — Labor-Managed Firm**  
A worker-owned enterprise where equity is derived from active participation. The operational unit of production under the IFMP. Characterized by democratic governance, distributed epistemic labor, and open-source innovation.

**LSQ — Labor-Saving Quotient**  
The proposed metric for capital allocation under the IFMP. Defined as the ratio of physical output gained per unit of labor-hour invested:

\[
LSQ = \frac{\Delta \text{Physical Output}}{\Delta \text{Labor Hours}}
\]

Intended to replace speculative ROI as the primary signal for investment.

**LTD — LVC Transition Dampener**  
A proposed cap on year-over-year LVC increases during the adoption transition. Proposed at 5% per year for the first T+5 cycles. Intended to prevent abrupt systemic shock during the transition period.

**LVC — Land Value Capture**  
A levy on the unimproved value of land and natural resource access points. Designed to socialize differential rent, eliminate speculative land-banking, and fund the Sovereign Development Reserve (SDR).

**LVT — Land Value Tax** *(See LVC)*

---

### M

**MCC — Mutual Credit Clearing**  
Cost-basis, interest-free financial intermediation via federated, member-owned clearinghouses. Credit is issued as a bilateral ledger entry against verified future production capacity. Priced at the marginal administrative cost of ledger maintenance (proposed 0.5%–1.0%).

**MGM — Multiplicative Geo-Mutualist Synthesis**  
*(Deprecated term; replaced by Mutualist Georgism. See entry below.)*

**Mutualist Georgism**  
The theoretical foundation of the IFMP. A unified framework that synthesizes Georgism (land-value socialization) and Mutualism (cost-basis credit, worker ownership) by recognizing that land-rent and interest are structurally identical forms of unearned extraction. Proposed as a complete system for post-rentier economic coordination.

---

### R

**RARR — Risk-Adjusted Reserve Ratio**  
The proposed reserve buffer for the Sovereign Development Reserve (SDR). Proposed at 200% of systemic output standard deviation (rolling 10-year window). Intended to buffer against supply shocks.

**ROI — Return on Investment**  
In the IFMP, ROI is restored to its thermodynamic function: a measure of resource efficiency rather than speculative yield. Expressed as the Labor-Saving Quotient (LSQ).

---

### S

**SDR — Sovereign Development Reserve**  
A federated reserve funded by LVC revenue. Used for:
- Maintenance of essential infrastructure
- Funding high-uncertainty, long-term TFP research
- Underwriting the Universal Basic Service (UBS) layer

**SGR — Strategic Global Reserve** *(Deprecated term; replaced by SDR)*

---

### T

**TFP — Total Factor Productivity**  
A measure of overall economic efficiency. The IFMP aims to maximize TFP by eliminating extractive friction (usury, land-rent, IP-hoarding) and enabling open-source replication of efficiency gains.

**TMC — Thermodynamic Marginal Cost**  
The proposed threshold at which sectoral decommodification is triggered. Proposed at 0.5% of average historical market price (normalized for labor-hour input). Intended to be regionally banded to account for local variation.

---

### U

**UBS — Universal Basic Service**  
Non-monetized provision of essential goods and services (energy, water, basic nutrition, transit, telecommunications, foundational housing). Financed by the SDR. Provides material security and eliminates coercion from participation in the wider economy.
