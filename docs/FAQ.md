# FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
## Integrated Federated Mutualist Protocol (IFMP)

**Document Type:** Reference / Frequently Asked Questions  
**Version:** 1.0 (Draft)  
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-13

---

### 1. HUMAN NATURE

**Q: Doesn't this system require people to be better than they are? Won't greed just re-emerge?**

**A:** The system does not rely on human virtue. It changes the incentive structure so that self-interest aligns with efficiency rather than extraction. In capitalism, greed drives rent-seeking. In Mutualist Georgism, greed drives TFP-maximization—because that's the only way to gain status, time-dividends, and agency. The machine doesn't care if the operator is selfish; it just responds to the torque you've set.

**The classism of the heart**—subjective prejudice, status-seeking, exclusionary preferences—cannot be eliminated, only regulated. The IFMP accepts this limitation and focuses on preventing structural oppression, not perfecting human nature.

---

### 2. INCENTIVES

**Q: If I can't get rich, why would I strive?**

**A:** The system offers three primary incentives:

1. **Time-Dividend:** Innovation returns efficiency gains as reduced working hours, not cash to idle owners. Everyone wants more time.

2. **Reputation and Status:** The DSM (Discretionary Social Market) provides a site for craft, cultural expression, and recognition. Excellence is rewarded with status.

3. **Agency:** Participation in governance offers meaningful influence over economic decisions—an option, not an obligation.

If none of these appeal, the UBS layer provides material security without requiring participation. Striving is optional; security is universal.

---

### 3. SLACKERS / FREE-RIDERS

**Q: What stops people from just doing nothing and living on the UBS?**

**A:** Nothing. And that's intentional. The UBS layer provides a baseline of material security—energy, water, nutrition, transit, housing. Those who choose not to participate are not a threat to the system because:

- They consume at sustainable levels.
- They don't destabilize governance (they don't participate).
- They don't create resentment (they're not seen as "drainers" because the system doesn't frame them as such).

The system only requires enough active participants to keep the engine running. The engine is so efficient that it doesn't require many.

---

### 4. DEMOCRATIC PARTICIPATION / EPISTEMIC OVERLOAD

**Q: Doesn't distributed governance require everyone to be an expert?**

**A:** No. The system is designed to function with modest levels of active engagement. Key features:

- **AI-Assisted Exception-Based Oversight:** Partners review anomalies flagged by algorithms, not raw data.
- **Optional Participation:** Agency is offered, not demanded. Those who participate gain influence; those who do not are not penalized.
- **ATD (Algorithmic Temporal Decay):** Prevents entrenchment by decaying decision-weight over time.

The system is robust to low participation. It does not require universal literacy or constant vigilance.

---

### 5. TRANSITION / IMPLEMENTATION

**Q: How do we get from here to there? Doesn't this require a revolution?**

**A:** No. The transition is already occurring through existing institutions:

- Community Land Trusts (CLTs) → proto-LVC
- Credit Unions and Mutual Banks → proto-MCC
- Worker Cooperatives (ESOPs) → proto-LMF
- Open-Source and Creative Commons → proto-Open-IP

The protocol proposes to **synchronize** these existing nodes into a federated network. No revolution is required—only gradual, competitive displacement through superior efficiency.

**Q: What if a rentier state suppresses IFMP nodes?**

**A:** The protocol employs "Asymmetric Jurisdictional Shadowing"—a distributed, multi-jurisdictional seeding strategy. Suppression in one jurisdiction strengthens the network by demonstrating resilience. The protocol is designed to survive even if nodes are suppressed, as it is not tied to a single geography or polity.

---

### 6. ANARCHISM / STATISM

**Q: Is this anarchist? Or statist?**

**A:** Neither and both. The protocol is **institutionally agnostic**. It can operate:

- Within a state (if the state adopts LVC, MCC, and LMF).
- Without a state (as a federated network of cooperatives).
- Or in any intermediate configuration.

**Civilian government is the ultimate arbiter.** The protocol is a tool for governments, not a replacement for them. It provides economic coordination; governments provide legitimacy and enforcement.

---

### 7. MARXISM / SOCIALISM

**Q: Is this just socialism with better marketing?**

**A:** No. Unlike socialism:

- **Markets remain** (for the DSM layer).
- **Private property exists** (in the DSM layer, for reproducible goods).
- **No central planning** (allocation is decentralized).
- **No State ownership** (LMFs are worker-owned, not state-owned).

What is abolished is **rentier extraction** (land-rent and interest), not markets or exchange. This is a post-rentier system, not a post-market system.

---

### 8. INCENTIVES FOR INNOVATION

**Q: If IP is abolished, who will invest in R&D?**

**A:** Open-source innovation has already demonstrated that it works—Linux, Wikipedia, and open-source AI are evidence. Under IFMP:

- **First-mover advantage** provides a temporary efficiency edge.
- **Reputational gains** accrue to innovators.
- **Time-dividends** return efficiency gains to producers.
- **SDR funding** supports high-uncertainty, long-term research.

The absence of IP does not mean the absence of incentive. It means incentives shift from *exclusive ownership* to *first implementation* and *reputation*.

---

### 9. COMPLEXITY / SCALE

**Q: How does this scale to a global economy?**

**A:** The protocol is designed to be **federated** and **scale-agnostic**:

- **LVC** is regional (land is inherently local).
- **MCC** is federated (clearinghouses can be local, regional, or global).
- **LMFs** are local (enterprises operate in specific communities).
- **SDR** is global (for large-scale R&D and infrastructure).

The system is modular. Nodes can join without disrupting existing nodes. Scale is emergent, not imposed.

---

### 10. THE "WHY" QUESTION

**Q: In a world of abundance and free time, what do people do?**

**A:** That's a philosophical question, not an economic one. The system provides the conditions for people to answer it themselves. Some will:

- Pursue craft and mastery in the DSM.
- Engage in governance and coordination.
- Explore creative, scientific, or spiritual work.
- Simply enjoy leisure, community, and family.

**This is the ultimate time-dividend.** The system does not prescribe a purpose. It only removes the barriers—material coercion, debt, and extraction—that prevent people from discovering their own.

---

### 11. POPULISM AND IMMIGRATION

**Q: How does the system handle immigration and demographic change?**

**A:** IFMP pools global surplus and distributes it to all labor, regardless of origin. Immigration is not zero-sum; it's positive-sum. More people = more innovation = more surplus = more time-dividends for everyone. Infrastructure is maintained through SDR funding. Cultural autonomy is preserved through the DSM. Populism cannot take root because the structural conditions for it don't exist.

---

### 12. THE CENTRAL BANK

**Q: Does IFMP abolish central banks?**

**A:** No. IFMP does not call for the abolition of central banks. It questions their efficiency and their necessity. Under MCC, the central bank's functions—interest rate setting, money supply control, inflation targeting, lender of last resort—become redundant. The strategy is not to abolish central banks, but to gradually render them irrelevant through competitive displacement. Central banks may continue to exist as coordinators, aggregators, and crisis managers—but their totalitarian role is dissolved.

---

### 13. AUTOMATION AND POST-LABOR

**Q: What if the system becomes so efficient it doesn't need any labor?**

**A:** That's the goal. When the system needs no labor, it has no scarcity. People are free to pursue meaning, connection, and creativity without coercion. UBS provides material security; DSM provides purpose; governance provides agency. This is not a problem to be solved; it's the ultimate feature—the ultimate time-dividend.

---

### 14. DOES IFMP "ABOLISH" THINGS? (New)

**Q: Isn't IFMP trying to abolish markets, money, and private property?**

**A:** No. IFMP does not abolish anything. It **redirects** extraction mechanisms that have been taken for granted.

| Extraction Type | Mechanism | IFMP Redirects To |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Wage Extraction | Employers capture surplus | LMF worker ownership |
| Resource Extraction | Landowners capture rent | LVC public use |
| Dividend Extraction | Financiers capture interest | MCC productive investment |
| Polity Extraction | Cross-border wealth capture | SDR global coordination |

Land is not abolished—its rent is redirected. Debt is not abolished—its repayment is redirected from interest to production. IP is not abolished—its exclusivity is redirected to shared innovation. Markets are not abolished—they are split into UBS (non-monetized essentials) and DSM (monetized discretion). Money is not abolished—it is confined to the DSM layer.

IFMP is a **redirection protocol**, not an abolition project.

---

### 15. EFFICIENCY VS. REDISTRIBUTION (New)

**Q: Is this just another redistribution scheme?**

**A:** No. Redistribution means taking value from one group and giving it to another. IFMP does not do this. It reduces friction so that value flows more efficiently to its sources.

Today, value is captured before it reaches producers. IFMP removes the capture mechanisms—not to "take from the rich," but to enable value to reach the people who create it.

The difference is critical:
- **Redistribution** is a moral claim ("this is fair").
- **Efficiency** is a systems claim ("this reduces friction").

Efficiency is testable. Redistribution is debatable. IFMP makes a testable systems claim.

---

### 16. DEFAULT RISK AND REPUTATION COLLATERAL (New)

**Q: How does IFMP handle default risk without interest?**

**A:** Through a cascading loss-absorption system:

1. **Firm-level:** Defaulting firm's credit is written down.
2. **Clearinghouse-level:** Losses beyond the firm's capacity are absorbed by its clearinghouse.
3. **Federation-level:** Systemic losses trigger SDR mutualization.

Access to credit is gated by **reputation collateral**—a firm's demonstrated production history and peer validation. This replaces interest-rate risk pricing with a non-extractive trust mechanism.

**Open Design Questions:** The exact rules for reputation decay (cumulative vs. time-decaying) and validator liability (none, individual, joint-and-several) are not specified in this draft. They will be resolved through simulation.

---

### 17. PEER VALIDATION AND VERIFICATION (New)

**Q: How does IFMP verify production claims without a central authority?**

**A:** Through **peer validation**—a distributed system where other LMFs and clearinghouses review production claims before MCC credit is issued. The SDR maintains an independent audit layer as a secondary check.

**Open Design Questions:** The exact liability structure for peer validators (whether they share joint-and-several liability, individual liability, or no liability) is not specified in this draft. The SDR audit function's funding independence (whether it is funded through LVC or independently) is also an open design question. These will be resolved through simulation.

---

### 18. THE GRADIENT AND "GREEN" (New)

**Q: Is "green" an endpoint? Does the system stop evolving?**

**A:** No. "Green" is a coordinate on a gradient—not an endpoint.

- UBS can continue to expand as new sectors cross the TMC threshold.
- DSM adapts as new cultural expressions and status signals emerge.
- TFP continues to improve as technology advances.

The gradient is dynamic. As technology evolves, the gradient shifts. As culture evolves, the gradient shifts. "Green" is a reference point for high-stability equilibrium, not a final state.
