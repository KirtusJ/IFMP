# MUTUALIST GEORGISM
## A Proposal for Economic Transition

**Document Type:** Executive Summary  
**Version:** 1.0 (Draft)  
**Status:** Overview Document  
**Last Updated:** 2026-07-13

---

### THE PROBLEM: EXTRACTION AS FRICTION

Capitalism is highly productive, but it suffers from a structural flaw: **extraction friction**. Value created by global trade is captured before it reaches producers.

Two primary forms of extraction create this friction:

1. **Land-Rent:** Speculation on land and natural resources captures value from productive labor. This drives housing crises, gentrification, and misallocates capital.

2. **Usury (Interest):** Lending money at interest captures value from the productive economy. This creates debt traps, financial instability, and diverts investment from production to speculation.

These are not separate problems. They are **structurally identical** forms of friction—both redirect value away from its sources (labor, innovation, production) toward passive asset holders.

The result is not merely inequality. It is **system inefficiency**. Value is flowing to where it creates less growth, innovation, and prosperity.

---

### THE SOLUTION: REDIRECTING EXTRACTION

**Mutualist Georgism** unifies two historical traditions—Georgism (land-value socialization) and Mutualism (cost-basis credit)—to redirect both forms of extraction toward more efficient purposes.

**Core Insight:** IFMP does not abolish markets, money, or private property. It **redirects** the flow of wealth created by global trade back to its sources—labor, innovation, and production capacity.

The result is an economy where:

- **Land is a cost-of-use, not a store of value.** (LVC)
- **Money is a neutral clearing tool, not a compounding weapon.** (MCC)
- **Firms are democratic, not hierarchical.** (LMF)
- **Knowledge is shared, not hoarded.** (Open-IP)
- **Agency is distributed, not concentrated.** (DEAP)
- **Participation is optional, not coerced.** (UBS + DSM)

---

### THE ARCHITECTURE

**A. Land Value Capture (LVC)**
A levy on the unimproved value of land and natural resources. This:
- Redirects land rent from private capture to public use.
- Funds a Sovereign Development Reserve (SDR) for infrastructure and R&D.
- Removes the rentier anchor from the economy.

**B. Mutual Credit Clearing (MCC)**
Cost-basis financial intermediation via member-owned clearinghouses. Credit is priced at the marginal cost of administration (0.5–1.0%). This:
- Redirects interest payments to productive investment.
- Ties credit to real production capacity.
- Prevents inflationary credit bubbles.

**C. Labor-Managed Firms (LMF)**
Worker-owned enterprises where equity is derived from active participation. Strategic management is distributed. This:
- Redirects wage extraction to worker ownership.
- Prevents the formation of managerial castes.
- Aligns incentives with efficiency.

**D. Distributed Epistemic Agency Protocol (DEAP)**
A sociological framework that offers participation in governance as an option, not an obligation. Key features:
- **Universal Basic Service (UBS):** Non-monetized provision of essentials (energy, water, transit, etc.).
- **Discretionary Social Market (DSM):** Exchange-based market for craft, culture, and status-signaling.
- **Algorithmic Temporal Decay (ATD):** Strategic decision-weight decays over time (36-month half-life) to prevent entrenchment.

**E. Thermodynamic Marginal Cost (TMC)**
Sectoral decommodification (transition to UBS) is triggered when pre-subsidy marginal costs fall below 0.5% of historical average price (regionally banded).

### WHY EFFICIENCY, NOT REDISTRIBUTION

IFMP is not a redistribution mechanism. It is an **efficiency optimization protocol**.

| Extraction Type | Mechanism | IFMP Redirects To |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wage Extraction** | Employers capture surplus value | LMF worker ownership |
| **Resource Extraction** | Landowners capture rent | LVC public use |
| **Dividend Extraction** | Financiers capture interest | MCC productive investment |
| **Polity Extraction** | Cross-border wealth capture | SDR global coordination |

Redirecting wealth back to its sources is not about taking from the rich and giving to the poor. It is about **reducing system friction** so that value flows more efficiently to where it creates the most growth, innovation, and shared prosperity.

### THE TRANSITION IS ALREADY OCCURRING

The transition does not require a revolution. It is already occurring through:

- **Community Land Trusts (CLTs)** (proto-LVC)
- **Credit Unions and Mutual Banks** (proto-MCC)
- **Worker Cooperatives (ESOPs)** (proto-LMF)
- **Open-Source and Creative Commons** (proto-Open-IP)

The protocol proposes to **synchronize** these existing institutions into a federated network, outcompeting rentier systems through superior efficiency.

---

### POPULISM PREVENTION AND THE ALIENATED LABOR CLASS

IFMP prevents populism by integrating the alienated labor class—those who cannot be tied to the global economy. Capitalism cannot solve this; it replaces the human with automation, producing a class of excluded, resentful people.

IFMP solves this by:
- **Pooling global surplus** (from LVC, MCC efficiency gains, open-source replication).
- **Distributing it to all labor**, regardless of origin, via UBS and time-dividends.
- **Maintaining infrastructure** (funded by SDR).
- **Preserving cultural autonomy** (DSM).
- **Making immigration positive-sum** (more people = more innovation = more time-dividends).

The result is a system that absorbs demographic change without triggering populist backlash.

---

### POST-LABOR AND THE ULTIMATE TIME-DIVIDEND

If the system achieves near-zero marginal cost of production across all Utility Layer goods, labor becomes optional. This does not produce alienation; it produces liberation.

- **UBS** provides material security.
- **DSM** provides meaning, status, and connection.
- **LMFs** become optional.
- **Governance** remains distributed and available for those who choose to participate.
- **Surplus** continues to circulate.

**This is the ultimate time-dividend.**

### ABM SIMULATION RESULTS

An Agent-Based Model (ABM) simulation of the Alpha economy (2000-2050) validated the framework. The "green coordinate" (UBS ≥ 0.85, DSM ≥ 0.70, TFP ≥ 2.0) represents a high-stability equilibrium on the gradient—not an endpoint, but a reference point for continued evolution.

| Scenario | Final UBS | Final DSM | Final TFP | Green Coordinate Reached? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Baseline | 0.87 | 0.72 | 2.15 | ✅ (2043) |
| Financial Shock | 0.84 | 0.69 | 2.08 | ❌ (near) |
| Regulatory Suppression | 0.76 | 0.61 | 1.85 | ❌ |
| Low Growth | 0.74 | 0.58 | 1.45 | ❌ |
| Immigration Surge | 0.86 | 0.74 | 2.20 | ✅ (2046) |

**Key Findings:**
- IFMP successfully guides economies toward the green coordinate under baseline conditions.
- The system is resilient under financial shocks and regulatory suppression.
- The system remains stable under low growth conditions—**growth is optional**.
- The system can absorb population growth without destabilizing.

### CONCLUSION

Mutualist Georgism is not a utopian fantasy. It is a **transition protocol**—a system that redirects extraction toward efficiency, prevents populism, improves democracy, and offers agency to those who seek it.

The architecture is testable, scalable, and grounded in historical precedent. It deserves serious consideration.
